Note: This interview is designed to be used as a phone screen and/or exploratory interview. As an exploratory interview it can be used for prospects who are not quite ready to fully apply for an open position. Generally these are top performers who are not looking or those who have multiple opportunities. The conversation starts off as a two-way dialogue, but if the candidate is strong and the job represents a career move, it shifts into more of a recruiting-oriented evaluation interview. You can modify the dialogue a bit for a basic phone screen.

Step 1 Starting Out Follow-up and Tips

Opening to establish framework of career- oriented discussion.

As discussed, this is an exploratory meeting to determine if the ________ posi- tion we have open represents a solid career move for you. I’m looking forward to this discussion. Would you mind giving me a super quick overview of your back- ground and current job-hunting status? I’ll then give you some insight into what we’re looking for. Based on that, we can decide next steps.

Have candidate give a 2-3 minute summary of current job situation and status of job- hunting efforts. Look for broad job fit and achiever pattern. Focus on breadth and focus of job.

What criteria would you use if you were to change jobs, or compare different jobs?

Look for balance between short- and long-term factors. Reinforce this point if there is balance. If not, point out that too much short-term focus is career limiting.

The first part of the exploratory interview is a two-way dialogue among peers. If the candidate seems like a good fit, the objec- tive is to look for areas of stretch or growth that could represent a career move.
DON’T GO INTO SALES MODE.

If the candidate is a strong person, find out the person’s job hunting status and what else the person is looking at. You’ll use this to position your opportunity in the form of a career move. The idea is to get the candi- date to sell you as to why he/she is qualified.
Step 2 Provide Job/Career Overview Follow-up and Tips

Present position in vague terms, looking for gaps in the candidate’s background that might represent career growth.

You have a background. I'm not sure our job is a perfect fit, but it might be. Let me give you a quick overview of the position we now have open and we can discus it.

Describe job in vague terms, highlighting areas that might offer growth opportunities.Consider strategic impact, team size, industry, company growth, importance of job, challenges, multi-functional exposure. The goal of this is to get the candidate to express some level of interest to see the position as a potential career move and one that is better than others being considered.

Based on this, what’s your general impression of this position as worthy of more consideration? What would you need to find out about the position as a next step?

It’s important that this presentation be done in comparison to what was learned during the previous review. Stay at a high level at the beginning. This allows you to figure out what the candidate perceives as a good career move.

Stay open-minded, looking for growth opportunities by comparing the candidate’s background to your opening. These are different for everybody depending on their aspirations and background, so get feedback from the candidate.

Step 3 Acknowledge, but Delay Impact of First Impression Information & Hot Tips

Stay aware of your biases.

Don’t be biased by your first impression of the person, good or bad. Instead, look for the achiever pattern: recognition, track record of performance, upward growth trend.
  • Wait 30 minutes
  • Stay objective throughout
Step 4 Review Work History and Background Fact-finding & Hot Tips

Look for pattern of success and the achiever pattern.

Find out pattern if person changed jobs.

Assuming the person is interested in your job, now it’s time to dig deeper into his/her background. During this review you’ll be validating if the person is a high achiever, determining if the person is a strong fit for your open job, and looking for gaps in the person’s background that your job fills. Alternatively, look for the high-quality person who sees your position as one offering high personal satisfaction.

Let’s review your background. I’d like to not only know what you’ve accomplished, but also see if our job offers you continued upward growth, or offers work you find extremely satisfying.

Spend 15-20 minutes on this. For each position obtain: title, promotions, basic duties, 360° team chart, impact made, challenges faced, any recognition received. Go back 5-10 years looking for upward trend of growth. Ask why the person changed jobs, looking for a career growth decision pattern. If the person is an achiever, but growth has stalled, or the current job is not highly satisfying, your job might be a good move.

Look for an Achiever pattern: major accomplishments in each job, upward progression, external recognition.

For each job obtain:

  • Overview, title, type of work
  • Draw org chart & how team was built
  • Dates, why left, explain gaps
  • Highlights, big projects, major focus
  • Recognition, raises, promotion

Look for gaps and voids like:

  • Project size and span of control
  • Influence and exposure
  • Impact and growth
Step 5 Present the Opportunity Follow-up and Tips

Present position as a career move. Be more specific, but don’t oversell.

If the candidate is an achiever with a strong background, you’ll want to be more specific in outlining your open position as a realistic career move.

I like your background quite a bit. You have accomplished a lot and should be proud of yourself. Let me give you a sense of what our job entails and see if it offers a strong career move for you.

Describe the job in more specific terms. Highlight the importance of the job and the impact it could have on the company or a major project. Tie to a strategic objective, if possible. Mention areas of immediate stretch in comparison to what the candidate has already done – team size, industry, company growth, budget, executive influence, and multi-functional exposure. Describe the longer term growth opportunities. The goal of this section is to get the candidate to express some level of interest and to see the position as a potential career move and one that’s better than others being considered.

Based on this, what’s your general impression of this position as a possible career move? Where do you see the growth opportunities for yourself? Is this something you’d want to consider in more depth? How does this compare to other opportunities you’re considering?

If the candidate isn’t looking, or has multiple opportunities, this overview is a critical step in recruiting the candidate.
DON’T OVERSELL.

Stay at a high level at the beginning, describing the generic reasons why the jobrepresents a strong career move for anyone.

Next, highlight some of the specific areasof growth opportunities – the gaps – for the candidate. Get feedback during the discussion. This allows you to figure out what the candidate perceives as a good career move. These are different for everybody depending on their aspirations and background.
Step 6 Most Significant Accomplishment (MSA) Question Tips & Quick Fact-finding

Determine job fit by getting examples of accomplishments related to performance profile.

If you and the candidate are both interested in moving to the next step, determine actual job fit using the MSA question. If time is available, ask this question for 2-3 accomplishments to observe trend of growth over time.

If you have about 10-15 minutes, I’d like to describe one of the biggest aspects of this job and get your thoughts. One of the key performance objectives for this position is _______ ( describe in some detail ).

MSA Anchor: Based on this, what have you accomplished that’s most similar to this? I like to spend a lot of time understanding this, so pick an accomplishment you’re very proud of, or one that best exemplifies some comparable work.

Note: Peel the onion and do a lot of fact-finding. Make sure you understand the accomplishment completely. You’ll use this information to assess the candidates and to defend them from those who conduct superficial interviews.

Career Decision Process

While I’ve seen a few other very strong candidates, I’m also very impressed with some of the work you’ve done. From my perspective, I’d like to continue the conversation. Is this something you’d like to pursue further?

Based on what you know about this position, how does it compare to others you’re considering, including your current position? What are the areas you find most appealing and what are those that appear less attractive? What criteria are you going to use to compare different opportunities and select one over the other?

If both of you are positive, describe next steps. Mention that you’ll be sending the person a formal form he/she should use to evaluate any position – the multi-factor career decision matrix. This form provides guidance to the candidate to compare jobs across multiple short- and long-term criteria such as job stretch, compensation, team, growth opportunities, etc. Mention that during the balance of the hiring process you’ll be providing the person this type of information in order to make the best career choice possible.
